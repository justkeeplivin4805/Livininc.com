How I Work

Decided well. Delivered exactly as decided.

Whether the spec is a piece of equipment or a business objective, the same failure shows up every time: what gets delivered quietly drifts from what was actually meant. LiViN exists to close that gap — on a purchase order, and on a strategic decision.

Capabilities

Procurement, executed like a banker would.

Diligence, sourcing, negotiation, and delivery — handled by one principal who's spent a career underwriting risk and matching capital to assets. Same instincts, applied to your spec sheet.

/ 01

Spec-First Sourcing

You give me the requirements. I match them — not a "close enough" substitute. Make, model, year, condition, certifications, region. If it isn't on spec, it isn't the answer.

/ 02

Off-Market Discovery

/ 03

Counterparty Diligence

/ 04

I negotiate the way deals are supposed to close — written, defensible, with clear terms. No verbal handshakes that fall apart at delivery.

/ 05

Logistics & Delivery

Inspection, transport, customs, last-mile. The asset arrives in the condition we agreed to, in the timeframe we agreed to, with the documentation you need to put it on a balance sheet or into the field.

/ 06

Single Point of Accountability

One contact, start to finish. No bait-and-switch, no handoffs to junior staff. The person you talk to is the person closing the deal.

Decision to Delivery

Most of the work happens before the decision gets made.

Twenty years underwriting risk taught me that a bad outcome is rarely a bad decision — it's a decision nobody defined clearly enough to hold onto. Working alongside executive leadership through a major strategic transformation confirmed it at a different altitude: technology, vendors, and implementation teams don't drift from a business objective because they're careless. They drift because the objective was never written down clearly enough to check against. I help leadership get that right at the start, then hold the line on it through execution.

// If it was decided, I make sure it's still true when it's built.
/ 01

Discovery

Before anything gets decided, I get underneath what's actually being solved for — the assumptions, the constraints, the stakeholders whose incentives don't yet agree. Ambiguity gets surfaced now, not six months into a build.

/ 02

Decision Criteria

A decision without written criteria isn't a decision — it's a guess with a deadline. I help leadership define, in writing, what a right answer actually has to satisfy before anyone evaluates an option against it.

/ 03

Mapped to the End Result

Every decision gets tied back to the outcome it's supposed to produce — explicitly, on paper — so that eighteen months and a dozen vendors later, anyone can still check delivery against intent.

/ 04

Held Through Execution

I don't run the project, design the architecture, or manage the vendors. I hold the record of what was decided and why — and I say so, plainly, the moment delivery starts to drift from it.

This isn't project management, change management, or strategy consulting — and it doesn't replace the executives, architects, or implementation teams doing the work. It's the same discipline this site already promises on a spec sheet: hold the standard from the moment it's set until the moment it's real. Industry-agnostic — the same practice applies in financial services, healthcare, government, manufacturing, or any organization making a decision it can't afford to lose track of.

Cross-Border · LATAM

American principal. US-origin equipment.
Delivered south of the border.

LATAM buyers know the value of US-origin equipment — quality, parts availability, resale floor. What's hard to find is a US-based principal who actually runs the deal end-to-end: spec-match, title verification, export documentation, payment structure, port delivery. That's where LiViN sits — and twenty years of banking is what makes the cross-border math hold up.

/ 01

Documented Payment Structures

Letters of credit, escrow, milestone wires — structured the way a bank expects to see them. Twenty years of finance applied to your security as much as ours.

/ 02

Export Documentation

Commercial invoice, certificate of origin, EEI filing, harmonized codes, EIN of record. Customs sees what they need; you get the paper trail you need on arrival.

/ 03

Port Delivery

FOB, CIF, or door-to-door — Miami, Houston, Long Beach, or direct to your destination port. Freight forwarders we trust, not whoever bid lowest that week.

/ 04

Principal-to-Principal

One contact, intake to delivery. No bait, no broker chain. The same person who quotes the deal signs the bill of lading.

Featured Practice · Healthcare into LATAM

Imaging, surgical, and lab equipment delivered into private clinics, hospitals, and imaging centers across Latin America.

Private healthcare across Mexico, Colombia, Chile, Brazil, and the broader region runs on US-origin imaging, surgical, and lab equipment — but the buying experience is broken. LiViN sources directly from credentialed US channels — refurbishers, decommissioning hospitals, OEM trade-ins — and delivers spec-matched units with full documentation, in clear USD terms, to your port.
